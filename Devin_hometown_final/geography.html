Geography

Explore the sections below to learn more about Lethbridge.

Lethbridge sits in the southern part of Alberta, only about an hour away from the U.S. border. One of the most recognizable things about the city is the Oldman River valley, which cuts through Lethbridge and forms the huge, rolling coulees that shape the landscape. These dramatic slopes are not only part of the city’s identity they’re also great places to walk, bike, and explore.

The University of Lethbridge notes that the city receives over 320 days of sunshine each year and often benefits from warm Chinook winds in the winter (“About Lethbridge,” University of Lethbridge). This climate makes the area bright, dry, and pleasant for most of the year. The surrounding terrain is mostly open prairie, but the Rocky Mountains can be seen in the distance, offering a stunning backdrop on clear days.

Thanks to the natural river valley and trail systems, Lethbridge is a perfect blend of city life and access to nature. You can enjoy shopping, sports, school, and restaurants and still be only minutes away from peaceful outdoor spaces.
